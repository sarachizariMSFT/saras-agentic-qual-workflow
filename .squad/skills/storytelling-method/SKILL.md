## Context
Used by the Storyteller in the reporting phase. Makes stakeholders feel the findings without bending the truth.
Every beat traces to a finding ID, and through it, to a transcript quote.

## Patterns

### The arc
`hook -> context -> tension (pains/papercuts) -> human moments (powerful moments) -> resolution (recommendations) -> call to action`
- **Hook:** open on a powerful moment (`MOM-*`).
- **Context:** who the users are and what job they came to do (JTBD framing helps).
- **Tension:** the pains and papercuts that got in the way, prioritized.
- **Human moments:** the emotional peaks that make it real.
- **Resolution:** what to change (recommendations) and the payoff if we do.
- **Call to action:** the decision you want from the room.

### Personas & journeys
- Evidence-backed only. If a persona is a composite, label it "illustrative" and cite the findings behind each trait.
- A journey map's every stage links to finding IDs for its friction/emotion.

### Highlight reel
- Script from Powerful-Moments' `clip_worthy` list: order the clips to follow the arc, with transcript_id + timestamp
  so `marvin-clipper` can cut them.

### Consistency with the report
- The Storyteller and Editor must not contradict. Same facts, different form. Reconcile before final sign-off.

## Examples
Opening beat: "P07 had been fighting the export for twenty minutes. Then it worked, and she laughed out loud
[MOM-001]. That relief is the whole story — and the reason the export flow is our top fix [REC-002]."

## Anti-Patterns
- Inventing a character, quote, or moment for drama. Cut it or find real evidence.
- A dramatic arc the evidence can't support — tell the quieter true story instead.
- A highlight reel clip with no timestamp.
